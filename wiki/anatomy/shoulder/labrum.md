# Glenoid Labrum

## Anatomy
- **Structure**: Redundant fold of joint capsule made of **fibrocartilaginous** tissue
- **Attachment**: Rim of the glenoid of the scapula
- **Function**: Deepens glenoid fossa; attachment site for long head of biceps tendon and [[glenohumeral-ligaments]]
- The labrum and ligamentous collagen fibers intertwine to form a strong histologic bond
- Injury is more likely at the **labrum-bone junction** (labral detachment) than at the labroligamentous junction

## MRI Appearance
- **Signal**: Uniformly low signal intensity on all pulse sequences
- **Best planes**:
  - **Superior labrum** — coronal oblique
  - **Anterior and posterior labrum** — axial
- **Shape variations** (all normal): Triangular (most common) > round > flat > absent
- May be affected by **magic angle phenomenon** → intrasubstance intermediate signal
- **Myxoid degeneration** with aging → globular intermediate signal

### Sequence Selection
- **FSE T2 FS**: Better labral evaluation when joint effusion is present
- **FSE PD FS** (or GRE): Better labral evaluation without effusion
- **MR arthrography**: Best overall for labral pathology

## Normal Variants

### Sublabral Foramen
- Anterosuperior labrum detached from glenoid
- Labrum **reattaches to glenoid at mid-portion**
- No clinical significance
- Key distinction from avulsed labrum: avulsed labrum will NOT reattach at mid-portion

### Buford Complex
- **Congenital absence** of anterosuperior labrum
- Associated with **markedly thickened middle glenohumeral ligament**
- The thick MGHL blends with anterior joint capsule on inferior axial images
- No clinical significance

### Sublabral Recess (Superior Sulcus)
- Synovial recess between glenoid rim and superior labrum
- **Key distinction from SLAP tear**: recess curves **medially with the glenoid** ("follows the flow")
- SLAP tears extend **laterally into the labrum** ("against the flow")

### Undercutting Cartilage
- Hyaline cartilage interposed between labrum and glenoid cortex
- Lower signal than gadolinium — do not confuse with labral detachment
- Also "follows the flow" of the glenoid contour

## Common Pathology
- [[bankart-lesion]] — anteroinferior labral tear (instability)
- [[slap-lesion]] — superior labral tear
- [[posterosuperior-impingement]] — posterior labral fraying/tear

## Imaging Pitfalls
- **MGHL** on axial images may mimic a torn anterior labral fragment
- Sublabral foramen mimics anterior labral tear
- Buford complex (absent anterosuperior labrum + thick MGHL) mimics pathology
- Superior sulcus mimics SLAP tear — use the "follow the glenoid contour" rule
- Myxoid degeneration mimics intrasubstance tear
