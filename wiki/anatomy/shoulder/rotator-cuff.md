# Rotator Cuff

## Anatomy
The rotator cuff is composed of four tendons that form a continuous tendinous hood:

| Tendon | Origin | Insertion | Function |
|--------|--------|-----------|----------|
| **Supraspinatus** | Supraspinous fossa | Superior greater tuberosity | Abduction (with deltoid) |
| **Infraspinatus** | Infraspinous fossa | Posterior greater tuberosity | External rotation |
| **Teres minor** | Lateral scapular border | Inferior greater tuberosity | External rotation |
| **Subscapularis** | Subscapular fossa | Lesser tuberosity | Internal rotation |

- **Innervation**: Supraspinatus and infraspinatus — suprascapular nerve; Teres minor — axillary nerve; Subscapularis — upper and lower subscapular nerves
- **Blood supply**: Suprascapular artery, anterior and posterior circumflex humeral arteries

## Structure
- Laminated structure: joint capsule + ligaments + four tendons
- Tendons have cylindrical and flat portions that fan out and **interdigitate** to form a continuous hood
- **No synovial sheaths** or paratenon surrounding the tendons
- **Coracohumeral ligament** lies superficial to the capsule and supraspinatus/infraspinatus tendons
- Extends from coracoid process to lesser and greater tuberosities and transverse humeral ligament

## MRI Appearance

### Supraspinatus
- **Best plane**: Coronal oblique (longitudinal view)
- Runs horizontally between undersurface of acromion and humeral head
- Inserts into **fibrocartilage** (not hyaline cartilage) on greater tuberosity
- Musculotendinous junction located just lateral to AC joint
- **Tip**: With internal rotation, bicipital groove is a landmark for the leading edge of supraspinatus

### Infraspinatus
- **Best plane**: Coronal oblique (posterior cuts)
- Courses obliquely craniocaudally at ~45 degrees
- Attaches to posterior portion of greater tuberosity

### Subscapularis
- **Best plane**: Axial (longitudinal view)
- Attaches to lesser tuberosity, runs anterior to shoulder joint
- Also well seen on sagittal oblique

### Teres Minor
- **Best plane**: Sagittal oblique (cross section)
- Located inferior to infraspinatus

## Normal Variants
- Variable thickness of tendons at insertion
- Magic angle effect can simulate tendon pathology (55-degree angle to B0 field)

## Common Pathology
- [[rotator-cuff-tear]] — partial and full-thickness
- [[shoulder-impingement]] — external and internal
- [[goutallier-fatty-infiltration]] — muscle degeneration grading
- Tendinosis — intermediate signal on all sequences without fluid-bright signal

## Imaging Pitfalls
- **Internal rotation** obscures anterior supraspinatus fibers near bicipital groove
- **Magic angle effect** at 55 degrees to main magnetic field — increased signal on short TE sequences (T1, PD) that disappears on long TE (T2)
- Partial volume averaging between biceps fluid and anterior supraspinatus on sagittal images
- Muscle atrophy assessment requires non-fat-suppressed T1W images
