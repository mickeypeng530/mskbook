# Plantar Fascia

## Normal MRI
- Low signal intensity on ALL sequences
- Thickness: should NOT exceed **4 mm** at proximal calcaneal attachment

---

## Plantar Fasciitis {#fasciitis}

### Demographics
- Runners
- Obese middle-aged women
- Seronegative spondyloarthropathy (**bilateral**)

### MRI Findings
- **Thickening** near calcaneal attachment (>4 mm)
- Intermediate T1W, **high T2W** signal
- **Perifasciitis**: edema surrounding fascia
- Bone marrow edema/erosions in plantar calcaneal tuberosity

---

## Plantar Fascia Rupture {#rupture}

### Mechanism
- Forced dorsiflexion

### Location
- **Midportion** (distal to typical fasciitis location at calcaneal attachment)

### MRI
- Discontinuity + surrounding high T2 signal

---

## Baxter's Nerve Entrapment {#baxter}

### Anatomy
- Inferior calcaneal nerve (first branch of lateral plantar nerve)
- Compressed as complication of chronic plantar fasciitis

### Denervation Pattern
Muscles affected:
- Abductor digiti minimi
- Flexor digitorum brevis
- Quadratus plantae

### MRI of Denervation
| Phase | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| **Early (acute)** | Increased T2W signal (edema) |
| **Chronic** | High T1W signal (**fatty atrophy**) |
