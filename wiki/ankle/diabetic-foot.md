# Diabetic Foot

## Marrow Signal Algorithm {#marrow-algorithm}

| T1W Signal | STIR Signal | Interpretation |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Normal fat (high)** | **Low** | **Normal** — osteomyelitis excluded |
| **High** | **High** | **Reactive marrow edema** from adjacent soft tissue inflammation |
| **Low** | **Low** | **Neuropathic** (chronic sclerosis) |
| **Low** | **High** | **Osteomyelitis** (if overlying ulcer at pressure point); or reactive edema; or acute neuropathic |

---

## Osteomyelitis {#osteomyelitis}

### MRI Findings
- Low T1W + high STIR marrow signal
- **Soft tissue ulcer adjacent to abnormal bone** in >90%
- Cortical destruction, periosteal reaction (nonspecific)

### Predictable Pressure Point Locations
- 1st and 5th metatarsal heads
- Calcaneal tuberosity
- Distal toes
- Malleoli

### Key Diagnostic Principle
- Osteomyelitis occurs at **pressure points** with overlying **skin ulcers**
- Low T1W + high STIR at a pressure point with adjacent ulcer = osteomyelitis until proven otherwise

---

## Neuropathic Arthropathy (Charcot) {#neuropathic}

### MRI Findings
- **Chronic**: low T1W + low STIR (sclerosis)
- **Acute**: low T1W + high STIR with bone fragmentation
- Always involves **joints** (most common: **tarsometatarsal joints**)
- NOT at pressure points (no overlying ulcer)
- Bone deformation: fragmentation, resorption

### Neuropathic vs Osteomyelitis

| Feature | Osteomyelitis | Neuropathic |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| Location | Pressure points | Joints (TMT) |
| Ulcer | Present (>90%) | Absent |
| Joint involvement | Secondary | Primary |
| Marrow (chronic) | Low T1/high STIR | Low T1/**low STIR** |

> **⚠️ Superimposed infection on neuropathic foot is IMPOSSIBLE to distinguish on MRI** — direct aspiration/biopsy required.

---

## Abscess vs Phlegmon {#abscess}

| Feature | Phlegmon | Abscess |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Enhancement | Diffuse, heterogeneous | **Peripheral** with non-enhancing center |

---

## Diabetic Muscle Changes {#muscle-changes}
- **Fatty replacement** (high T1W) + **edema** (high STIR) — denervation/ischemia

> **⚠️ Pitfall**: Do NOT misinterpret denervation changes as **pyomyositis**.

- Achilles and PTT frequently torn in diabetics (microvascular ischemia)

---

## Joint Fluid
- Does NOT diagnose septic joint (reactive effusions common in diabetic foot)
- Use MRI to **direct aspiration/biopsy** rather than to make definitive diagnosis
