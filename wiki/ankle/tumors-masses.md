# Ankle & Foot Soft Tissue Tumors & Masses

## Plantar Fibromatosis {#plantar-fibromatosis}
- Unique to foot; benign fibrous proliferation **within plantar fascia**
- Location: usually medial; usually painless
- MRI: nodular thickenings of plantar fascia
  - T1W: low to intermediate signal
  - T2W: **low to intermediate** signal (abundant collagen)
  - Enhancement: common but not invariable
- Margins: upper = **infiltrative**; lower = **well-defined** (outlined by fat)
- Surgery/biopsy only for large lesions with typical appearance

---

## Synovial Sarcoma {#synovial-sarcoma}
- **Most common malignant** soft tissue tumor of the foot
- Extra-articular; young adults
- Calcifications on XR in ~20%
- MRI: nonspecific; can mimic benign cystic lesion with very high T2W signal
- Key features: isointense to slightly hyperintense to muscle on T1W (unlike fluid); abnormal enhancement
- **Include in differential** for nondescript foot mass in young patient

---

## Other Soft Tissue Tumors

### Benign
- Ganglion cysts
- Hemangiomas
- Lipomas
- Nerve sheath tumors (see [[nerves#tarsal-tunnel]])
- GCT of tendon sheath (extra-articular PVNS)
- Soft tissue chondroma
- [[#plantar-fibromatosis]]

### Malignant
- [[#synovial-sarcoma]] (most common in foot)
- Liposarcoma, MFH: rare below knee; tend to occur at ankle level

## Accessory Muscles (Tumor Mimickers) {#accessory-muscles}

MRI appearance is **diagnostic** — signal identical to normal muscle on all sequences.

| Muscle | Location | Clinical Significance |
|--------|----------|----------------------|
| **Accessory soleus** | Medial/posterior ankle, ventral to Achilles | Exercise-related ischemia (compartment syndrome); tarsal tunnel compression |
| **Peroneus quartus** (13-25%) | Posterior to Achilles, lateral | Lateral ankle pain, peroneal subluxation, simulates split tear |
| **Accessory FDL** | Tarsal tunnel | Compressive neuropathy of posterior tibial nerve |
| **Peroneocalcaneus internus** | Parallels FHL | Simulates FHL longitudinal split |

## Pressure Lesions / Adventitial Bursae {#pressure-lesions}
- Round lesion in subcutaneous fat at pressure points
- T1W: low to intermediate; T2W: vague intermediate/high; fat intermixed
- May develop cystic center (high T2W)

### Common Locations
- Plantar to 1st/2nd/5th metatarsal heads
- Medial to 1st metatarsal head (hallux valgus)
- Plantar to calcaneal tuberosity
- Posterior to distal Achilles

> **⚠️ Transfer lesions**: After foot surgery, pressure lesions develop at new weight-bearing sites.

---

## Foreign Bodies {#foreign-bodies}
- Most not radiopaque; may migrate from entry site
- MRI: linear, **low signal on T1W and T2W**
- Surrounding inflammation much more conspicuous than the foreign body itself (high T2 signal)
