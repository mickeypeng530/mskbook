# Ankle Nerve Abnormalities

## Tarsal Tunnel Syndrome {#tarsal-tunnel}

### Tunnel Boundaries
- **Craniocaudal**: medial malleolus → navicular bone
- **Lateral wall**: talus + calcaneus (sustentaculum tali)
- **Medial wall**: flexor retinaculum + abductor hallucis muscle

### Contents
- Posterior tibial nerve
- Posterior tibial artery/vein
- PTT, FDL, FHL tendons

### Clinical
- Burning, paresthesias in sole and toes (worse with activity)
- Motor symptoms: late manifestation

### Causes of Compression (most common on MRI)
1. **Ganglion cysts**
2. **Nerve sheath tumors** (schwannomas)
3. FHL tenosynovitis
4. Tarsal coalition (middle facet bone hypertrophy)
5. Accessory muscles (accessory soleus, accessory FDL)
6. Venous varices
7. Hemangioma
8. Pannus (inflammatory)
9. Post-traumatic fibrosis

### Ganglion vs Nerve Sheath Tumor
Both appear: homogeneous low T1, high T2

| Feature | Ganglion Cyst | Nerve Sheath Tumor |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|
| Enhancement | **Peripheral only** | **Solid** enhancement |
| Surgery | Simple excision | Nerve-sparing excision |

> **⚠️ Gadolinium needed** to differentiate — surgery is significantly different.

> **⚠️ If MRI is negative**: surgery not indicated (scarring/fibrosis is the cause, not detectable by MRI)

## Morton's Neuroma (Interdigital Neuroma) {#morton}

### Pathology
- Chronic plantar digital nerve entrapment → perineural fibrosis + neural degeneration + intermetatarsal bursitis
- NOT a true neuroma

### Location
- **2nd or 3rd intermetatarsal web space**

### Demographics
- Young to middle-aged women; high-heeled shoes

### MRI Appearance
- Teardrop-shaped mass between metatarsal heads, projecting **inferiorly** into plantar fat
- **T1W**: intermediate signal
- **T2W**: usually **LOW signal** (abundant fibrosis) — key differentiator from bursitis

### Morton's Neuroma vs Intermetatarsal Bursitis

| Feature | Morton's Neuroma | Bursitis |
|---------|-----------------|----------|
| T2 signal | **LOW** (fibrosis) | **HIGH** (fluid) |
| Shape | Teardrop, plantar | Runs **vertically between** metatarsal heads |
| Enhancement | Yes (but not needed) | Peripheral |

> **⚠️ Note**: Morton's neuroma can be present in **asymptomatic** patients — clinical correlation essential.
