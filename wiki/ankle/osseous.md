# Ankle & Foot Osseous Abnormalities

## Tarsal Coalition {#tarsal-coalition}

### Epidemiology
- ~6% of population; males 4x more; **bilateral in ~50%**
- Congenital (failure of segmentation) or acquired (RA, trauma)
- Subtypes: osseous, fibrous, cartilaginous, or combined

### Two Most Common Types

| Type | Location | Symptoms | Best Imaging |
|------|----------|----------|-------------|
| **Calcaneonavicular** | Calcaneus–navicular bridge | Often **asymptomatic** | Medial oblique XR |
| **Talocalcaneal** | **Middle facet** (sustentaculum tali–talus) | Typically **symptomatic** | MRI coronal |

### Talocalcaneal Coalition MRI {#middle-facet}
- Narrowing, irregularity, or osseous fusion of middle facet
- Joint angle often inferiorly directed
- **Osseous**: complete fusion, no joint visible on T1W coronal
- **Fibrocartilaginous**: irregular narrowed joint + bone marrow edema on **both sides**
- Associated: secondary DJD of posterior subtalar joint, displaced PTT/FHL tendons, peroneal/extensor spasm, flatfoot

### Accessory Navicular

### Hallux Sesamoids {#sesamoids}
- Located in flexor hallucis brevis tendons at 1st metatarsal head
- **Medial sesamoid**: more likely **traumatic** (fracture, stress fracture)
- **Lateral sesamoid**: more likely **osteonecrosis**
- MRI is sensitive but nonspecific — differential diagnosis usually required

### Turf Toe {#turf-toe}
- Excessive dorsiflexion at 1st MTP joint → disruption of plantar capsular tissues ("plantar plate")
- Sesamoid dislocation/subluxation may occur

## Osteochondral Lesions of Talus (OLT) {#olt}

- Medial or lateral dome with **equal frequency**
- Associated ligamentous injuries virtually always present

### MRI Signs of Instability (Loose Fragment)
1. **High T2 signal surrounding fragment**
2. Large subchondral cysts deep to fragment
3. Crack in overlying cartilage
4. Absent or displaced fragment ± intra-articular loose body

> **⚠️ Pitfall**: Large subchondral cysts may mimic bone tumor — document articular surface communication.

---

## Fractures {#fractures}

### General MRI Signs
- Linear low signal T1W with linear high or low T2W signal
- Fractures generally **perpendicular** to long axis of bone

> **⚠️ Exception**: Medial malleolus stress fractures are **vertically oriented**

---

## Osteonecrosis {#osteonecrosis}

### Common Locations in Foot/Ankle
1. **Navicular** (unrecognized fracture)
2. **2nd metatarsal head** — **Freiberg's infraction** (high-heeled shoes)
3. **Lateral hallux sesamoid**
4. **Talar dome** (after talar neck fracture — disrupted blood supply to proximal talus)
5. Anywhere (steroids)

### MRI
- Serpiginous low signal lines (geographic pattern)
- Diffuse low T1W without serpiginous lines → DDx: osteonecrosis, occult fracture, stress injury, osteomyelitis, bone marrow edema syndrome

---

## Bone Marrow Edema Syndrome {#bmes}

- Patchy increased T2 signal scattered throughout **multiple** foot/ankle bones
- Unknown etiology; all ages but more frequent in young
- Often **bilateral**; **self-limiting** (months to ~1 year)
- NOT chronic regional pain syndrome
- No biopsy needed; conservative treatment
- Similar to idiopathic transient osteoporosis of hip

## Bone Tumors by Location {#bone-tumors}

| Location | Typical Tumors |
|----------|---------------|
| Distal tibia/fibula | NOF, GCT, ABC |
| Calcaneus neck | Simple bone cyst (unicameral); intraosseous lipoma |
| Calcaneus tuberosity | ABC, chondroblastoma, GCT |
| Talus/calcaneus | Osteoid osteoma (extensive edema) |

### Bone Tumor Mimickers
| Location | Mimicker | Key |
|----------|----------|-----|
| **Medial malleolus** | Intraosseous ganglion cyst | Typical location |
| **Talar dome** | Post-traumatic subchondral cyst | May be very large; document articular communication |
| **Mid calcaneus** (sinus tarsi) | Vascular remnants | Incidental, asymptomatic, normal variant |
| **Calcaneus neck** | "Lipoma" | Likely resolving unicameral bone cyst with fatty evolution |
