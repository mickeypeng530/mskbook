# Ankle Ligaments

## Normal Appearance
- Thin, taut, low signal intensity structures
- **Striated appearance is NORMAL** for: anterior tibiofibular, PTFL, deep tibiotalar (deltoid), tibiocalcaneal
- Do NOT confuse striations with tears

---

## Medial Ligaments (Deltoid Complex) {#deltoid}

Best seen on **coronal** images.

### Components
| Ligament | Course | Best View | Notes |
|----------|--------|-----------|-------|
| **Deep tibiotalar** | Oblique: medial malleolus → talus | Coronal + axial | Striated (normal) |
| **Tibiocalcaneal** | Vertical: to sustentaculum tali | Coronal | Thinner |
| **Talonavicular** | Medial malleolus → navicular | Coronal | — |
| **Spring ligament** | See below | Coronal + axial | Medialmost deltoid component |

### Deltoid Tear MRI
- **Deep tibiotalar**: loses striated appearance, high signal on T1W and T2W
- **Tibiocalcaneal**: discontinuous + high signal (acute); thickened/discontinuous (chronic)
- Deltoid injuries **much less common** than lateral ligament injuries

### Spring Ligament (Plantar Calcaneonavicular) {#spring-ligament}
- J-shaped; arises from medial malleolus → runs inferiorly deep to [[tendons#ptt]] → supports **head of talus** like a sling → inserts on sustentaculum tali and navicular
- Function: critical for arch support together with PTT
- Three portions described; typically not identified separately on MRI

#### Spring Ligament Pathology
- Abnormal: thickened, intermediate T1/T2 signal, or gap (tear)
- **Strongly associated with PTT tears** — when PTT fails, spring ligament cannot support arch alone

> **⚠️ Pitfall**: Normal gap/increased signal in plantar medial aspect of spring ligament — do NOT call tear.

## Lateral Ligaments {#lateral}

### Superior Group (Syndesmotic Ligaments) {#syndesmosis}
- **Anterior tibiofibular ligament** (AITFL)
- **Posterior tibiofibular ligament** (PITFL)
- Located just above tibiotalar joint; best on **axial** images

> **⚠️ Pitfall**: PITFL on sagittal images mimics intra-articular **loose body** when surrounded by joint fluid.

### Inferior Group (Lateral Collateral Ligaments)

| Ligament | Key Features |
|----------|-------------|
| **ATFL** | Most commonly torn; thickening of ankle capsule; at level of malleolar fossa |
| **CFL** | Most difficult to identify; best on coronal images |
| **PTFL** | Striated (normal); rarely torn |

### Sequential Tearing Order
**ATFL → CFL → PTFL** (progressive lateral ankle injury)

### ATFL Tear MRI {#atfl-tear}
- **Acute**: torn fragments + hemorrhage/edema leaking into soft tissues (capsular tear)
- **Chronic**: irregular, thickened, thinned, or detached — may appear intact (scarring)

### Lateral Ankle Sprains
- **80-90%** of all ankle ligament injuries
- **15%** result in instability
- Associated findings:
  - [[impingement#sinus-tarsi]] syndrome
  - [[impingement#anterolateral]] impingement
  - [[tendons#peroneal-split]] (peroneus brevis split)
  - [[osseous#olt]] (osteochondral lesions of talar dome)
