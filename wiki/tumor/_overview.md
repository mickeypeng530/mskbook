# Tumor MRI Overview

## Imaging Modalities — Role of Each

| Modality | Role | Strength |
|----------|------|----------|
| **Radiograph** | **FIRST LINE** — obtain early, even if MRI planned | Most SPECIFIC for characterization |
| **MRI** | Most SENSITIVE; best for LOCAL STAGING | Appearance often nonspecific |
| **CT** | Matrix characterization (calcification, osteoid); cortical destruction | Better than MRI for calcification |
| **Bone scan** | Screen for multifocal disease | Can be negative; MRI more sensitive |

### MRI vs CT Comparison
- **MRI superior**: Intramedullary extent, soft tissue extension, NV bundle, skip lesions, epiphyseal involvement
- **CT superior**: Cortical destruction, periosteal reaction, calcification, sclerotic changes
- **Equal**: Joint involvement (both >90% specificity)
- Key stats: Intramedullary extent correlation with pathology — MRI r=0.99 vs CT r=0.86

## Standard Protocol

### Setup
- Position: Supine (standard)
- **Initial**: Body coil, large FOV → survey for entire tumor + skip lesions
- **Detailed**: Surface coil for margins, NV structures, joints
- Skin marker over suspected mass; postop: image entire scar length

### Sequence Protocol

| Seq | Type | Plane | FOV | Purpose |
|-----|------|-------|-----|---------|
| 1 | STIR | Cor/Sag | Large | Initial survey; sensitive for tumor, edema, skip lesions |
| 2 | T1W | Cor/Sag | Large | Intraosseous extent (sharp contrast vs fat) |
| 3 | T1W | Axial | Small | Tumor margins, anatomy, NV relationship |
| 4 | STIR/FS-T2W | Axial | Small | Extraosseous extent, tissue characterization |
| 5 | T1W or FSE-T2W | Sag/Cor | Small | Longitudinal plane tangential to lesion |

### Contrast Guidelines
- Standard tumor protocol: Gadolinium **NOT routinely required**
- **USE Gd**: Cystic vs cystic-appearing solid; postop/postradiation follow-up
- If using fat-sat post-Gd T1W → **MUST obtain pre-contrast fat-sat T1W in SAME PLANE** (avoid pseudoenhancement)

### Pitfall: FSE-T2W
- Fat remains bright on FSE-T2W → may mask intramedullary lesions
- **ALWAYS use fat saturation** with FSE-T2W for tumor imaging

## Enneking Staging System {#enneking}

### Components
- **G** (Grade): G1 = Low grade, G2 = High grade (determined by biopsy)
- **T** (Local extent): T1 = Intracompartmental, T2 = Extracompartmental
- **M** (Metastases): M0 = None, M1 = Present

### Benign Stages
| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 (Latent) | G0, T0, M0 — inactive, encapsulated |
| 2 (Active) | G0, T0, M0 — progressive growth, intracapsular |
| 3 (Aggressive) | G0, T1-T2, M0 — may penetrate capsule/cortex |

### Malignant Stages
| Stage | Grade | Local Extent | Metastases | Prognosis |
|-------|-------|-------------|------------|-----------|
| **IA** | G1 (Low) | T1 (Intra) | M0 | Best |
| **IB** | G1 (Low) | T2 (Extra) | M0 | Good |
| **IIA** | G2 (High) | T1 (Intra) | M0 | Moderate |
| **IIB** | G2 (High) | T2 (Extra) | M0 | Poor |
| **III** | Any G | Any T | M1 | Worst |

- NOT applicable to: Leukemias, myelomas, Ewing sarcoma (for staging), metastases
- Each joint = separate compartment; articular invasion changes stage

## MRI Staging Checklist {#staging-checklist}

| Feature | Best Sequence | Key Points |
|---------|--------------|------------|
| **Intraosseous extent** | T1W + STIR | STIR may overestimate (edema); T1 shows sharp contrast with fat |
| **Extraosseous extent** | T2W/STIR | Edema: feathery margins, no mass effect, no distortion |
| **NV involvement** | T1W axial + T2W | Clear fat plane = no involvement |
| **Joint invasion** | T1W + T2W | Effusion is NOT reliable sign; absence = good negative predictor |
| **Skip lesions** | Large FOV STIR | Non-contiguous foci in same bone; 25% of osteosarcomas |
| **Adenopathy** | T1W + T2W | Same poor prognosis as distant metastases |
| **Measurements** | — | 5-cm cuff needed; reference to osseous landmark |

### Peritumoral Edema vs Tumor
- Both show high signal on post-Gd T1 → cannot distinguish on standard sequences
- Dynamic contrast-enhanced MRI: Viable tumor initial slope >20% higher than edema

## Benign vs Malignant Features

| Feature | Typically Benign | Typically Malignant |
|---------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Margins | Smooth, well-defined | Irregular, ill-defined |
| Signal | Homogeneous | Heterogeneous |
| NV involvement | Absent | Present |
| Edema | Minimal/absent | Extensive |
| Dynamic enhancement | Slope <30% (72%) | Slope >30% (84%) |

### CRITICAL WARNING
- **SIGNIFICANT OVERLAP** between benign and malignant appearances
- **DANGEROUS** to determine benign vs malignant based on MRI alone
- Standard post-Gd T1W NOT helpful for differentiating
- **ALWAYS correlate with radiographs** — they provide most specific information
- T1/T2 relaxation time measurements are UNRELIABLE

## Post-Treatment Evaluation {#post-treatment}

### Post-Chemotherapy
- Responder: <10% viable tumor cells → good prognosis
- Non-responder: >10% viable tumor cells → poor prognosis
- MRI limitations: Changes in size/signal/edema NOT reliably predictive
- Dynamic enhancement: Residual tumor enhances faster than reactive tissue
- Enhancement slope drop ≥60% = response; <60% = non-responder

### Pan Classification (Post-Chemo MRI Patterns)
| Pattern | T1 | T2 | Interpretation |
|---------|----|----|----------------|
| **Dark** | Low | Low | Good response, small residual |
| **Mottled/Speckled** | Intermediate | Scattered high/low | Low residual viable |
| **Homogeneous** | Intermediate | High, no mottling | Macroscopic residual viable |
| **Cystic** | Intermediate | Multicystic/bubbly high | High proportion peripheral viable |

### Post-Surgery/Radiation
- **No high T2/STIR signal** → strong predictor of non-recurrence
- High T2 + no mass → likely edema/hemorrhage (look for fatty marbling, intact fascial planes)
- **Texture sign**: Normal skeletal muscle architecture on T1W despite T2 signal → highly predictive of non-recurrence
- Mass with peripheral-only enhancement → seroma, lymphocele, abscess
- **Enhancing mass → RECURRENCE LIKELY** → biopsy indicated
- Magnetization transfer contrast (MTC): Scar >50% effect vs tumor <25%

## Structured Report Template {#report}

1. Lesion identification (location, size in 3D)
2. Signal characteristics (T1, T2/STIR, homogeneity, fat, matrix)
3. Margins (well-defined vs ill-defined, capsule)
4. Intraosseous extent (with landmark reference: "X cm from articular surface")
5. Extraosseous extent (dimensions, compartments)
6. NV structures (clear fat plane vs encasement)
7. Joint involvement (distance, intra-articular extension)
8. Skip lesions (body coil survey)
9. Lymphadenopathy
10. Enhancement pattern (if contrast given)
11. Impression (diagnosis/DDx, staging summary, recommendations)
