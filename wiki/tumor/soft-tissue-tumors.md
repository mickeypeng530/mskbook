# Soft Tissue Tumors

## General Principles
- Some soft tissue tumors diagnosed with certainty on MRI signal pattern
- Large overlap between benign and malignant — most indeterminate masses should be **BIOPSIED**
- For nonspecific appearance: Use patient **age**, **location**, and differential tables

---

## High T1 Signal Masses {#high-t1}

High T1 usually indicates **FAT** or **subacute blood** (methemoglobin)

### Lipomatous Tumors

| Lesion | T1 | T2 | Key Features |
|--------|----|----|-------------|
| **Lipoma** | HIGH (fat) | Inter-High | Parallels subcutaneous fat on ALL sequences; thin septa; suppresses on STIR |
| **Atypical lipoma / WD liposarcoma** | Predominantly HIGH | Variable | Predominant fat + **coarse, thickened septa** (>2 mm); non-fatty enhancing tissue |
| **Myxoid liposarcoma** | LOW-Inter | Very HIGH | May appear **CYSTIC**; often NO recognizable fat; lobulated + septations |
| **High-grade liposarcoma** | Variable | Variable | Often NO fat; indistinguishable from other sarcomas |

- **Lipoma vs WD liposarcoma**: Suspect malignancy if >5 cm, non-fat components, thick septa >2 mm
- Gadolinium NOT necessary to diagnose fat-containing lesions (fat-sat confirms)
- Liposarcoma grade inversely proportional to fat content

### Vascular Lesions

| Lesion | T1 | T2 | Key Features |
|--------|----|----|-------------|
| **Hemangioma (cavernous)** | Iso + HIGH foci (fat) | Very HIGH | Lobular; fat content; low foci (phleboliths/thrombus); may be infiltrative |
| **AV malformation** | Flow voids + fat | Flow voids | Large high-flow vessels = dark flow voids; HIGH on GRE |

### Other High T1

| Lesion | Key |
|--------|-----|
| **Hematoma (subacute)** | HIGH (methemoglobin); heterogeneous, laminated; chronic = hemosiderin rim |
| **Melanoma** | Paramagnetic compounds → T1 shortening; variable appearance |

### Hematoma vs Hemorrhagic Tumor
- Can be **INDISTINGUISHABLE**
- ANY hematoma must be **followed to resolution** (clinically or serial imaging)
- Gadolinium may show enhancing mass, but organizing hematoma also enhances
- If questionable → image-guided **BIOPSY**

## Low T2 Signal Masses {#low-t2}

| Lesion | T1 | T2 | Key Features |
|--------|----|----|-------------|
| **PVNS** | Inter-Low | **LOW** (hemosiderin) | Synovial proliferation; knee most common; GRE **"blooming"**; evaluate entire joint |
| **GCT of tendon sheath** | Inter-Low | Heterogeneous, low foci | Extra-articular PVNS; hand/wrist most common; adjacent to tendon |
| **Fibrous lesions** | Low-Inter | Low-Inter | Plantar fibroma, Morton's neuroma, desmoid tumors |
| **Amyloid** | Low-Inter | Low-Inter | Hip/shoulder; associated with renal dialysis |
| **Gout (tophi)** | Low-Inter | Low-Inter | ± bone erosions; fibrous tissue/hemosiderin/calcification |
| **Melanoma** | May be HIGH | May be LOW | Paramagnetic; variable |
| **Chronic hematoma** | Variable | LOW | Peripheral hemosiderin rim |

### PVNS
- DDx: Chronic hemarthrosis, RA, TB arthritis, amyloidosis, gout
- Entire joint must be evaluated for additional foci

### Desmoid (Extra-Abdominal Aggressive Fibromatosis)
- Age 15-40; thigh/upper arm/buttock
- Poorly demarcated, infiltrates neighbors, does **NOT metastasize**; recurrence 25-68%
- T2: Cellular areas high signal; fibrous fascicles = linear/nodular **signal voids** (if prominent → can suggest diagnosis)
- Other forms: Dupuytren (palmar), Ledderhose (plantar), Peyronie (penile)

## Cystic-Appearing Masses {#cystic}

### True Cyst Features
- T1: Homogeneous LOW
- T2: Diffusely HIGH
- Internal septa: Thin, low signal
- Post-Gd: **Thin peripheral wall/septa ONLY; NO central enhancement**

### Differential Diagnosis

| Lesion | Enhancement | Key |
|--------|-------------|-----|
| **Ganglion/synovial cyst** | Peripheral only | Typical locations (popliteal fossa, dorsal wrist) |
| **Intramuscular myxoma** | Peripheral/septal ± internal | Well-circumscribed, gelatinous; age 50-70; associated with fibrous dysplasia |
| **Myxoid liposarcoma** | Internal | Solid tumor with myxoid elements |
| **Myxoid chondrosarcoma** | Internal | Myxoid matrix |
| **Synovial sarcoma** | Diffuse internal | Often appears cystic but diffuse enhancement proves solid |
| **Nerve sheath tumor** | Diffuse | Target sign, split-fat sign, nerve entering/exiting |

### Algorithm
- Atypical location + absence of classic pure cyst features → **USE GADOLINIUM**
- **Internal enhancement** → solid component → consider **BIOPSY**

## Nerve Sheath Tumors {#nerve-sheath}

### General
- Most common age 20-50; schwannoma typically solitary; neurofibroma may be multiple (neurofibromatosis)
- Grow slowly; freely movable **perpendicular** to nerve axis, NOT along axis

### MRI Signs
- **Nerve continuity**: Tubular nerve entering and exiting mass (longitudinal images)
- **Split-fat sign**: Peripheral rim of fat surrounding lesion
- **Target sign** on T2: High signal periphery (myxomatous) + low signal center (fibrocollagenous)
- Internal fascicles on T2
- Diffuse enhancement

### Plexiform Neurofibroma
- Arises from large nerve trunks; wormlike mass
- **Malignant transformation signs**: Large size, indistinct outline, infiltration, severe heterogeneity, rapid growth
- MRI **cannot reliably distinguish** benign from malignant nerve sheath tumor

## Specific Malignant Soft Tissue Tumors

### MFH (Malignant Fibrous Histiocytoma)
- **Most common** malignant soft tissue tumor in adults; average age 50
- Low-to-intermediate T1, heterogeneous high T2, strong enhancement
- If fibromatous cells predominate → **low T2** signal
- Recurrence rate up to 45%

### Synovial Sarcoma
- Origin: Soft tissues (NOT synovial lining); <10% intra-articular
- Young adults; lower extremity
- Calcifications along periphery in 1/3
- MRI: Nonspecific; lobulated, septations, fluid levels

### Rhabdomyosarcoma
- From striated muscle; subtypes: Embryonal, alveolar, pleomorphic
- MRI: Nonspecific — low T1, high T2

### Angiosarcoma
- Rare; may arise on chronic lymphedema (Stewart-Treves syndrome post-mastectomy)

## Specific Benign Soft Tissue Tumors

### Intramuscular Myxoma
- Age 50-70; thigh, shoulder, upper arm
- Sharply marginated within muscle; low-inter T1, strongly increased T2 (cyst-like)
- **Heterogeneous enhancement** (unlike true cyst) — key differentiator
- Association with fibrous dysplasia (multiple myxomas)

### Hemangioma (Soft Tissue)
- Children: Usually skeletal musculature with atrophy
- MRI: High T1/T2 (fatty stroma); serpiginous dilated vessels; phleboliths = signal void

### GCT of Tendon Sheath
- Histologically identical to PVNS; flexor tendons of fingers
- Low T1; heterogeneous T2 (low = fibrotic/hemosiderin; high = inflamed synovium)
- Recurrence 9-20%; malignant transformation can occur
- See also [[tumors-masses]] (wrist chapter)

## Diagnostic Pitfalls Summary {#pitfalls}

| Pitfall | Consequence | Prevention |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Relying on MRI alone for bone tumor | Misdiagnosis | **ALWAYS correlate with XR** |
| Aggressive-appearing benign lesions | Unnecessary aggressive treatment | Know: osteoid osteoma, chondroblastoma, stress fracture, LCH |
| Assuming benign/malignant on MRI margins | Missed malignancy | Significant overlap → biopsy when uncertain |
| Overestimating extent on STIR | Overcalling stage | Correlate with T1W; edema ≠ tumor |
| FSE-T2 without fat-sat | Missed intramedullary lesion | Always fat-sat with FSE-T2W |
| Pseudoenhancement | False positive | Pre-contrast fat-sat T1W in same plane |
| Cystic-appearing malignancy | Missed sarcoma | Gd for atypical cystic mass; internal enhancement = solid |
| Biopsy of stress fracture | Histology mimics malignancy | Follow with XR in 2-3 weeks |
| Granulation tissue vs recurrence | False positive | Texture sign; biopsy if uncertain |

## Key Points
- High T1 soft tissue mass: Fat (lipoma/liposarcoma) or blood (subacute hematoma)
- Low T2 on both T1 and T2: PVNS, GCT-TS, fibrous lesions, amyloid, gout
- Cystic-appearing mass + internal enhancement = **solid tumor** → biopsy
- Nerve sheath: Target sign + split-fat sign + nerve entering/exiting
- Lipoma vs liposarcoma: >5 cm + thick septa >2 mm + non-fat components → suspect malignancy
- Any hematoma: Follow to resolution; if not resolving → biopsy
- MFH: Most common malignant ST tumor in adults; can have low T2 if fibrous predominant
