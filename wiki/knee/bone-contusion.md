# Bone Contusion & Soft Tissue Injuries

## Bone Contusions

### General
- **Amorphous, subarticular high signal on T2W** images
- Essentially **microfractures** — heal with rest
- Significance:
  1. Can be **sole source of pain**
  2. May precede focal **osteochondritis dissecans**
  3. Specific patterns indicate **associated internal derangements**
- **Geographic** contusion → higher risk of collapse/fragmentation than reticular pattern → protect from weight-bearing

### Contusion Patterns

| Pattern | Contusion Location | Mechanism | Associated Injury |
|---------|--------------------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Pivot-shift** | Posterolateral tibial plateau + central-anterior lateral femoral condyle | Tibial internal rotation on femur | **ACL tear** |
| **Contra-coup** | Medial femoral condyle + posterior medial tibial plateau | Medial impaction after ACL tear instability | **Peripheral meniscal tear**, meniscocapsular separation |
| **Patellar dislocation** | Anterior lateral femoral condyle ± medial patella | Patella dislocates laterally / reduces | [[patellofemoral]] — check cartilage |
| **Hyperextension** | Anterior femoral condyle + anterior tibial plateau | Direct impaction | — |

- Pivot-shift pattern without ACL tear can occur in **children** (increased ACL flexibility protects from tearing)

## Soft Tissue Injuries

### Tennis Leg
- Acute, activity-related **calf pain** (classically in tennis players)
- May present with swelling and **purplish skin discoloration** (hemorrhage)
- Previously attributed to plantaris tendon tear; now known to more commonly result from **partial tearing of medial gastrocnemius**
- Clinically resembles **deep venous thrombosis**
- MRI: Abnormal signal within medial gastrocnemius + focal **fluid collection between gastrocnemius and soleus**
- True plantaris rupture: Torn, retracted tendon ± tubular fluid collection

### Morel-Lavallée Lesion
- Fluid collection at **subcutaneous fat–fascia interface** along anterior knee
- Mechanism: **Shearing/degloving injury** — fat sheared off fascia attachment
- Most commonly seen in **gluteal region**; high recurrence rate
- May mimic distended prepatellar bursa, but fluid extends **farther** (medially, laterally, or proximally) than typical for bursa

---

## Key Points
- Bone contusion = microfracture; geographic pattern needs protection from weight-bearing
- Posterolateral tibial plateau contusion → look for ACL tear (pivot-shift)
- Contra-coup pattern → peripheral meniscal tear / meniscocapsular separation
- Patellar dislocation contusion → check cartilage for surgical decision
- Tennis leg: Medial gastrocnemius tear, NOT plantaris; clinically mimics DVT
- Morel-Lavallée: Degloving at fat-fascia interface; extends beyond typical bursa boundaries
