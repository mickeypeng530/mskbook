# Knee Bursae

## Popliteal (Baker's) Cyst
- Most common knee bursa
- Actually a **joint recess** extending posteriorly between **medial gastrocnemius** and **semimembranosus** tendons
- Small amount of fluid can be normal (proportional to joint fluid)
- When distended → source of symptoms; can become very large
- Complications:
  - **Hemorrhage**
  - **Compartment syndrome**
  - **Distal extension** into lower leg
  - **Rupture** → surrounding tissue inflammation → mimics **deep vein thrombosis**

---

## Prepatellar Bursa
- Common cause of anterior knee pain
- Caused by repetitive trauma from kneeling ("housemaid's knee")
- Easy clinical diagnosis — rarely seen as isolated finding on MRI
- MRI: Fluid collection **superficial to patella**

---

## Pes Anserinus Bursa
- Location: **Anteromedial tibia**, just below joint line
- "Pes anserinus" = goose's foot (Latin) — webbed configuration of tendons
- Pes tendons: **Gracilis, sartorius, semitendinosus**
- Bursa lies beneath tendons, superficial to MCL
- When inflamed: Extends **proximally toward joint**

---

## Semimembranosus–Tibial Collateral Ligament Bursa
- Can mimic internal derangement
- Location: **Posteromedial joint line**
- Characteristic appearance: Drapes over semimembranosus tendon like a **horseshoe**
- On coronal/sagittal: Appears to arise at meniscus and extend inferiorly → may mimic **parameniscal cyst**

---

## Medial Collateral Ligament Bursa
- Uncommon
- Location: Just **deep to MCL**, extends vertically above and below joint line
- Can be confused with **meniscocapsular separation**
  - Distinguishing feature: Fluid is **well-contained and cystlike** (vs. diffusely distributed in true separation)

---

## Summary: Medial Bursae Locations

All four bursae described occur **medially** in distinct locations:

| Bursa | Location | Key Feature |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **Baker's cyst** | Posterior (medial gastrocnemius–semimembranosus) | Joint recess; rupture mimics DVT |
| **Pes anserinus** | Anteromedial, below joint line | Superficial to MCL; extends proximally |
| **Semimembranosus-TCL** | Posteromedial joint line | Horseshoe around tendon; mimics parameniscal cyst |
| **MCL bursa** | Deep to MCL, vertical | Cystlike; mimics meniscocapsular separation |

- When markedly distended, bursae may overlap typical locations → use **axial images** for differentiation
- Name is less important than **recognizing the bursa** and communicating it to the clinician

---

## Key Points
- Baker's cyst: Most common; rupture mimics DVT
- Prepatellar: Kneeling injury; fluid superficial to patella
- Pes anserinus: Anteromedial, below joint line, superficial to MCL
- Semimembranosus-TCL: Horseshoe shape at posteromedial joint line
- MCL bursa: Deep to MCL; cystlike — distinguish from meniscocapsular separation
