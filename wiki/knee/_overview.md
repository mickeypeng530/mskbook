# Knee MRI Overview

## Coil & Position
- Dedicated surface coil
- FOV: 14-16 cm, slice thickness 4 mm, interslice gap 0.3-0.4 mm
- Knee in ~5° external rotation (relaxed position) — aligns ACL parallel to sagittal plane

## Sequences

| Sequence | Plane | Purpose |
|----------|-------|---------|
| FSE PD FS | Sagittal | **Menisci** (short TE essential), cartilage |
| FSE T2 FS | Sagittal | Cruciate ligaments, cartilage, bone |
| FSE T2 FS | Coronal | Collateral ligaments, cruciates (second look), bone |
| FSE T2 FS | Axial | Patellar cartilage, cruciates/collaterals (third look) |

### Key Technical Points
- **Short TE** (PD, T1, or GRE) is mandatory for meniscal evaluation — long TE loses intrameniscal signal → missed tears
- Fat suppression on short TE improves meniscal tear conspicuity
- **TE 40-60** is ideal for articular cartilage assessment
- GRE: Good for cartilage/menisci but **unacceptable for bone** (poor marrow pathology demonstration)
- STIR can substitute for FSE T2 FS
- No routine IV gadolinium; MR arthrography only for postoperative meniscus evaluation
- T1 useful for marrow: Normal red marrow should be brighter than skeletal muscle

## Systematic Review Checklist
- [ ] Joint effusion / popliteal cyst
- [ ] Medial meniscus (body segments → anterior/posterior horns)
- [ ] Lateral meniscus (body segments → anterior/posterior horns)
- [ ] ACL and PCL
- [ ] MCL and LCL complex
- [ ] Quadriceps and patellar tendons
- [ ] Articular cartilage (all compartments)
- [ ] Osseous structures (contusions, fractures)
- [ ] Patellofemoral joint
- [ ] Bursae
