# Cofield Rotator Cuff Tear Size Classification

## Overview
Classification of **rotator cuff tear size** based on the greatest dimension of the tear. Used for surgical planning and prognosis of [[rotator-cuff-tear]] repair.

## Original Reference
- Cofield RH. Subscapular muscle transposition for repair of chronic rotator cuff tears. Surg Gynecol Obstet. 1982;154:667-672.

## Classification Table

| Size | Greatest Dimension | Description |
|------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Small** | < 1 cm | |
| **Medium** | 1-3 cm | |
| **Large** | 3-5 cm | |
| **Massive** | > 5 cm | Usually involves multiple tendons |

## MRI Criteria
- Measure the **greatest dimension** of the tendon defect
- Assess in **coronal oblique** (anteroposterior extent) and **sagittal oblique** (craniocaudal extent) planes
- Also assess **tendon retraction** — distance from insertion to retracted tendon margin

## Clinical Application
- Larger tears → higher risk of retear after repair
- Massive tears (> 5 cm) often involve multiple tendons with significant retraction and muscle atrophy
- Combined with [[goutallier-fatty-infiltration]] grading to determine surgical candidacy
- Small/medium tears: Primary repair usually feasible
- Massive tears with significant atrophy: May require tendon transfer, superior capsular reconstruction, or reverse total shoulder arthroplasty

## What to Report
1. Tear size (greatest dimension)
2. Which tendons are involved
3. Degree of tendon retraction
4. Muscle atrophy/fatty infiltration ([[goutallier-fatty-infiltration]])
5. Quality of tendon margins
