# Goutallier Fatty Infiltration Classification

## Overview
Grading system for **fatty degeneration of rotator cuff muscles**, originally described on CT and adapted for MRI. Important for **preoperative planning** — severe fatty infiltration predicts poor surgical outcome for rotator cuff repair.

## Original Reference
- Goutallier D et al. Clin Orthop Relat Res. 1994;304:78-83.

## Classification Table

| Grade | Criteria | Clinical Significance |
|-------|----------|----------------------|
| **0** | No fatty streaks | Normal |
| **1** | Some fatty streaks | Minimal degeneration |
| **2** | Less fat than muscle | Moderate degeneration |
| **3** | Equal fat and muscle | Significant — may affect repair outcome |
| **4** | More fat than muscle | Severe — poor prognosis for surgical repair |

## MRI Criteria
- Assessed on **T1W images** (fat is bright)
- **Best planes**: Sagittal oblique (cross-sectional view of all four muscles) and coronal oblique
- Fat appears as **high signal intensity within muscle** on T1W
- Compare ratio of fat to muscle tissue within the muscle belly
- **Important**: Muscle atrophy assessment is easily missed on fat-suppressed images — always include at least one non-FS T1W sequence

## Clinical Application
- Used in **preoperative planning** for [[rotator-cuff-tear]] repair
- Higher Goutallier grade → higher risk of:
  - Failed surgical repair
  - Retear after repair
  - Poor functional outcome
- Grade 3-4 may be considered **irreversible** — may favor conservative treatment or tendon transfer over primary repair

## Pitfalls
- Fat-suppressed images obscure fatty infiltration — use non-FS T1W
- Grading is somewhat subjective — inter-observer variability exists
- Assess each muscle individually (supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor, subscapularis)
