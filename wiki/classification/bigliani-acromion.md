# Bigliani Acromion Morphology Classification

## Overview
Classification of **acromial morphology** based on the shape of the undersurface of the acromion on sagittal oblique images. Type III (hooked) acromion is most associated with [[shoulder-impingement]] and [[rotator-cuff-tear]].

## Original Reference
- Bigliani LU et al. The morphology of the acromion and its relationship to rotator cuff tears. Orthop Trans. 1986;10:228.

## Classification Table

| Type | Morphology | Clinical Significance |
|------|-----------|----------------------|
| **I** | **Flat** | Lowest association with impingement |
| **II** | **Curved** | Intermediate association |
| **III** | **Hooked** | **Highest association** with impingement and cuff tears |

## MRI Criteria
- Assessed on **sagittal oblique** images
- Evaluate the undersurface (inferior surface) of the acromion
- Type I: Flat undersurface
- Type II: Smooth, curved undersurface (concave inferiorly)
- Type III: Anteroinferior hook or spur projecting downward

## Clinical Application
- Type III acromion narrows the subacromial space
- Increased risk of supraspinatus tendon impingement and tears
- May be considered during surgical planning for subacromial decompression (acromioplasty)
- Os acromiale (unfused acromial apophysis) also contributes to impingement — best seen on axial images

## Pitfalls
- Grading can be subjective, especially Type II vs. III
- Osteophytes on the undersurface of the acromion or AC joint may coexist and contribute to impingement independent of native acromial shape
- Os acromiale can be confused with AC joint on axial images — look for separate ossification center
