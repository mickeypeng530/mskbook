# Wrist & Hand MRI Overview

## Coil & Position
- Dedicated surface coil — smaller coil = better images
- Challenge: Getting wrist near **isocenter** of magnet
  - Smaller patient: Supine, arm alongside body
  - Larger patient: Prone, arm overhead, elbow flexed (uncomfortable — pad well)
- FOV: ~**10 cm** (wrist), ~14 cm (hand), 6-8 cm (finger)

## Image Orientation
Based on **axial scout** through proximal carpal row → true anatomic planes

## Sequences

### Routine Wrist Protocol

| Seq | Type | Plane | Slice | Purpose |
|-----|------|-------|-------|---------|
| 1 | T1 | Coronal | 3 mm | Osseous, marrow |
| 2 | GRE (T2*) | Coronal | **1-2 mm** | **Ligaments** (thin slices essential) |
| 3 | Turbo T2 | Coronal | 3 mm | Fluid-sensitive |
| 4 | GRE (T2*) | Axial | 3 mm | Tendons, nerves |
| 5 | T1 | Axial | 3 mm | Tendons, carpal tunnel |
| 6 | T1 | Sagittal | 3 mm | Alignment, pisotriquetral joint |

### Key Technical Points
- **Coronal 1-2 mm** slices mandatory for intrinsic ligament evaluation
- 3D GRE volume acquisition excellent for ligaments (very thin slices)
- 3 mm acceptable in other planes and for non-ligament pathology
- **Contrast**: IV gadolinium for mass (cystic vs solid) and infection (abscess delineation)
- **MR arthrography**: Radiocarpal injection; not frequently used in routine practice

### Special Protocols
- **Mass/Infection**: T1 + STIR (coronal/axial/sagittal) → then post-Gd T1 FS (coronal/axial)
- **Trauma screening**: T1 + T2 FS/STIR coronal only (2 sequences)
- **Gamekeeper's thumb**: T1 + GRE coronal, FOV 6-8 cm

### Coronal

- Extrinsic ligaments

### Axial
- [[tendons|Tendons]] (flexor and extensor)
- Median nerve / [[carpal-tunnel|carpal tunnel]]

- Distal radioulnar joint

### Sagittal
- Carpal alignment (DISI/VISI)
- Pisotriquetral joint
