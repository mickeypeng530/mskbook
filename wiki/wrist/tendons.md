# Wrist & Hand Tendons

## Normal Anatomy

### Flexor Tendons
- **Nine tendons** pass through carpal tunnel on volar aspect
- Unnecessary to know individual flexor tendon names for wrist MRI
- Normal: Oval-to-round, low signal on axial images
- Small amounts of fluid in tendon sheaths = **normal** (abnormal only if fluid completely surrounds tendon)

### Extensor Tendons — Six Dorsal Compartments {#dorsal-compartments}
Stabilized by extensor retinaculum with fascial septations:

| Compartment | Tendons | Landmark |
|-------------|---------|----------|
| **I** | Abductor pollicis longus (APL), extensor pollicis brevis (EPB) | Radial side |
| **II** | Extensor carpi radialis longus (ECRL), extensor carpi radialis brevis (ECRB) | Radial to Lister's |
| **III** | Extensor pollicis longus (EPL) | **Ulnar to Lister's tubercle** |
| **IV** | Extensor digitorum, extensor indicis | Dorsal |
| **V** | Extensor digiti minimi | Dorsal-ulnar |
| **VI** | Extensor carpi ulnaris (ECU) | **Notch of ulna** |

### Mnemonic for Longus/Brevis
Starting at EPL (ulnar to Lister's), going radially, they **alternate**: EPL → ECRB → ECRL → EPB → APL

### Normal Variants / Pitfalls
- **APL striations**: Fat interposed between tendinous fascicles mimics longitudinal tears — normal
- **ECU intrasubstance signal**: Some high signal is normal; tenosynovitis only if fluid completely surrounds tendon

## de Quervain's Syndrome {#dequervain}
- **First dorsal compartment** entrapment/tenosynovitis (APL + EPB)
- Associated with overuse (manual laborers) and **pregnancy**
- Clinically can mimic: Scaphoid fracture, FCR tenosynovitis, 1st CMC DJD

### MRI Findings (Variable)
- Obliteration of subcutaneous fat around tendons
- Intermediate signal tissue around tendons on all sequences, OR
- Tenosynovitis with T2 high signal fluid
- Tendons: Normal caliber, thickened, or high intrasubstance signal (partial tear/degeneration)

### Treatment
- Steroid injection into tendon sheath cures most patients
- Surgical decompression occasionally required

---

## Intersection Syndrome {#intersection}
- Overuse peritendinosis where **1st compartment crosses over 2nd compartment**
- Location: **4-8 cm proximal to Lister's tubercle** (must scan proximally in forearm!)
- Activities: Skiing, weight lifting, rowing, shoveling, raking
- Often overlooked clinically — MRI useful for diagnosis

### MRI
- Tenosynovitis of 1st and 2nd compartment tendons
- Abnormal peritendinous signal at level of tendon crossover, extending proximally

### Distal Intersection Syndrome
- EPL crosses ECRL/ECRB **distal** to Lister's tubercle

---

## Extensor Carpi Ulnaris (ECU)
- Commonly affected by tenosynovitis or partial tears
- May occur from repetitive subluxation/dislocation after TFCC injury
- Diagnose tenosynovitis/tear only when:
  - Fluid **completely surrounds** tendon, OR
  - Tendon abnormally thick or thin
- Subluxation: ECU displaced medially from groove on dorsal ulna (axial images)
- See also [[tfcc#components]]

---

## Bowstringing
- Rupture of finger **pulley** → flexor tendons displace volarly from phalanges
- MRI: Increased distance between tendon and bone compared to normal
- Best seen when finger slightly **flexed** in coil
- Compare with adjacent normal digit

---

## Tendon Tears (General)
- Complete tears accurately assessed with MRI
- Gap > **30 mm** → typically requires **tendon graft** (not primary repair)
- Tenosynovitis of flexor tendons in carpal tunnel = common cause of [[carpal-tunnel|CTS]]
- Infection spreads rapidly along tendon sheaths — always consider **purulent tenosynovitis** in DDx

---

## Key Points
- Six dorsal compartments: APL/EPB, ECRL/ECRB, EPL, ED/EI, EDM, ECU
- de Quervain's: 1st compartment; mimics scaphoid fracture clinically
- Intersection syndrome: 4-8 cm proximal to Lister's; must extend FOV proximally
- ECU: Don't overcall intrasubstance signal; supination subluxation = normal
- Tendon gap >30 mm → graft needed
- APL striations = normal fat between fascicles, not tears
