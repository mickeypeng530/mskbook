# Wrist & Hand Tumors and Masses

## Ganglion Cyst (Most Common Wrist Mass)
- **Fibrous-walled mass** containing thick mucoid fluid (petroleum jelly-like)
- Attached by pedicle to tendon sheath, joint capsule, ligament, or fascial plane
- Usually women in their 30s; cause uncertain (chronic irritation)
- May erode adjacent bone
- **Occult dorsal SL ganglion**: Common source of dorsal wrist pain; clinically not palpable — always check dorsal SL ligament carefully

### MRI
- **T1**: Low signal (occasionally higher if high protein)
- **T2**: Diffusely high signal
- **Characteristic feature**: Thin **septations** (low signal lines within mass on T2)
- **Post-Gd**: Enhancement of fibrous wall and thin septations; no enhancement of cyst contents

---

## Intraosseous Ganglion Cyst
- Common in carpal bones, especially **radial aspect of lunate**
- Mechanism: Often from small ganglion in SL ligament eroding into bone
- Subchondral location; may be confined to bone or extend from soft tissue ganglion
- MRI: Small, rounded, well-circumscribed; low T1, high T2
- Can show lesion when radiographs are normal

---

## Giant Cell Tumor of Tendon Sheath (GCT-TS)
- **Second most common** soft tissue mass of hand/wrist
- Extra-articular, localized form of **PVNS** (pigmented villonodular synovitis)
- Most commonly: Volar aspects of fingers

### MRI
- **Low signal on BOTH T1 and T2** (limits DDx significantly)
- DDx for low T1 + low T2 soft tissue mass: **Amyloid deposits**, **gouty tophi**
  - Gout: Multiple lesions + joint involvement
  - Amyloid: Systemic process from known underlying disease

---

## Glomus Tumor
- Benign tumor from neuromyoarterial glomus bodies
- Glomus bodies concentrated in **fingertips** (especially beneath fingernails)
- Location: Usually **dorsal margin of distal phalanx** (occasionally volar)
- Symptoms: Severe aching pain, point tenderness, **cold sensitivity**

### MRI
- Small, well-defined; low T1, **hyperintense T2**
- **Strong enhancement** after contrast
- Thin low signal capsule on all sequences
- Best seen on **axial and sagittal** images
- May cause bone erosion (not seen on XR)
- MRI valuable: Establish diagnosis, detect multiple lesions, guide surgery (lesions are tiny)

---

## Enchondroma
- Cartilaginous rests within bone; lobulated margins; may erode endosteal cortex
- Location: **Proximal and middle phalanges**, metacarpals
- Often contain calcifications (visible on XR, hard to identify on MRI)
- MRI: Low T1, high T2; lobulated; characteristic location
- Usually **incidental findings**; get XR when confused

---

## Anomalous Muscles
- Common in hand and wrist
- May present as soft tissue mass or cause nerve compression
- **Extensor digitorum manus brevis**: Most common; dorsum of wrist, ulnar to extensor indicis
- Clinically confused with ganglion cyst
- MRI: Mass **isointense to muscle** on all sequences, predictable location

---

## Pisotriquetral Synovial Cyst
- Enlarged synovial recess of pisotriquetral joint
- Often associated with pisotriquetral DJD (ball-valve mechanism)
- Similar concept to Baker's cyst in knee
- Volar wrist mass, just proximal to pisiform
- Low T1, high T2; report as symptomatic cyst when ≥ **1 cm**
- Treatment: Aspiration + steroid injection; surgical pisiform removal if refractory

---

## Key Points
- Ganglion cyst: Most common; septations on T2 are characteristic; check dorsal SL for occult source
- Intraosseous ganglion: Radial lunate; from SL ligament degeneration
- GCT-TS: Low signal on T1 AND T2 — DDx: amyloid, gout
- Glomus tumor: Fingertip, cold sensitivity, strong enhancement; tiny — MRI guides surgery
- Anomalous muscle: Isointense to muscle on all sequences
