# Triangular Fibrocartilage Complex (TFCC)

## Overview
- **Primary stabilizer** of the distal radioulnar joint (DRUJ)
- Located on ulnar side of wrist
- Functions: Cushion axial loading forces (ulnar side absorbs ~**20%**) + stabilize DRUJ

## Components {#components}
1. **Triangular fibrocartilage** (TFC)
2. **Dorsal and volar radioulnar ligaments**
3. **Meniscus homologue**
4. **Extensor carpi ulnaris (ECU) tendon sheath**
5. **Ulnar collateral ligament** (wrist)

---

## Triangular Fibrocartilage (TFC)

### Anatomy
- **Biconcave disk** with asymmetric bow-tie shape (similar to TMJ disk)
- Radial attachment: Via hyaline cartilage on ulnar aspect of radius (do NOT confuse cartilage with tear/detachment)
- Ulnar attachment: Two thin bands to ulnar styloid process
- Best depicted on **coronal** images
- Normal signal: Diffusely low, or intermediate intrasubstance (asymptomatic myxoid degeneration)
- **Thickness inversely proportional to ulnar variance**:
  - Positive ulnar variance → thinner TFC → predisposed to tear
  - Negative ulnar variance → thicker TFC

### TFC Tears
- Symptoms: **Ulnar-sided wrist pain**, tenderness, audible click with forearm rotation
- Evaluation similar to knee meniscus:
  - Intrasubstance signal = **no clinical significance**
  - High signal (equal to fluid) extending through proximal or distal **surface** on T2 = **tear**
- Partial tears: More frequent at **proximal surface** (facing DRUJ)
- Full-thickness tears: Extend completely through TFC

### Vascularity & Treatment Implications
- **Peripheral 20%** (ulnar margin): Well vascularized → may heal with immobilization or primary repair
- **Central and radial portions**: Avascular → tears debrided if symptomatic

### TFC Detachment
- Traumatic detachment from ulnar attachment
- May become interposed between radius and ulna → prevents DRUJ reduction
- Often associated with injuries to ECU tendon sheath and LT ligament

### MRI Accuracy
- **95%** compared with arthrography and surgery
- Central/radial tears: Best shown
- Ulnar attachment tears: Historically less accurate (synovitis/prestyloid recess proliferation can mimic or obscure); improved with modern techniques

## Radioulnar Ligaments

### Anatomy
- Broad, striated bands from ulnar styloid → distal radius along volar and dorsal margins of sigmoid notch
- Blend with volar/dorsal surfaces of TFC
- Distinguished from TFC: **Flat superior/inferior margins** (vs biconcave TFC) + attach directly to **bone** (vs cartilage)
- Low signal on all sequences; best seen on coronal images

### Tear
- Disruption → **DRUJ instability**
- Malalignment best evaluated on **axial** images
- Ulna displaced dorsally or volarly relative to sigmoid notch of radius

---

## Meniscus Homologue
- Thickening of ulnar joint capsule; **inconsistently present**
- Located just distal to prestyloid recess; attaches to triquetrum
- Coronal: Low signal, **triangle-shaped** structure
- **Prestyloid recess**: Rounded space bordered by meniscus homologue distally, TFCC attachments proximally, TFC radially; normally contains fluid

---

## Extensor Carpi Ulnaris (ECU) Sheath

### Normal
- Best depicted on **axial** images
- Tendon in groove on **dorsum of ulna** (neutral/pronation)
- **Pitfall**: Tendon may normally subluxate if imaged in **supination**
- ECU may normally have some high intrasubstance signal — do NOT call abnormal unless:
  - Fluid completely surrounds tendon (tenosynovitis), OR
  - Tendon abnormally thick or thin

### ECU Sheath Disruption
- Traumatic disruption → medial subluxation/dislocation of ECU from its groove
- Associated tenosynovitis common
- Best evaluated on **axial** images

---

## Ulnar Collateral Ligament (Wrist)
- From ulnar styloid → triquetrum
- Thickening of wrist joint capsule; provides **little mechanical strength**
- Radial side has analogous radial collateral ligament (radial styloid → scaphoid)

---

## Key Points
- TFC tear: Signal must reach surface on T2 (same principle as meniscus)
- TFC thickness ∝ 1/ulnar variance; positive variance → thinner → more tears
- Peripheral 20% (ulnar) = vascular → reparable; central/radial = avascular → debride
- Radioulnar ligament tear → DRUJ instability; check axial for subluxation
- ECU: Don't overcall — intrasubstance signal and supination subluxation are normal
