# Gamekeeper's Thumb (Skier's Thumb)

## Anatomy
- **Ulnar collateral ligament (UCL) of thumb**: Taut band from base of proximal phalanx → distal 1st metacarpal
- Stabilizes ulnar aspect of 1st MCP joint
- MRI: Low signal band spanning 1st MCP joint, located **deep to adductor aponeurosis**
- Requires **high-resolution, small-FOV coronal images** oriented to thumb

---

## Mechanism
- **Abduction injury** to 1st MCP joint

## Injury Types
- **With avulsion fracture**: ~1/3 of cases
- **Ligament-only injury**: ~2/3 of cases (no radiographic abnormality)

---

## MRI Findings

### Simple UCL Tear (Non-Stener)
- UCL avulsed from base of proximal phalanx or torn within substance
- Hemorrhage and edema surrounding torn ends
- Ligament remains **deep to** adductor aponeurosis

### Stener Lesion {#stener}
- UCL retracted **proximally** and displaced **superficial to** adductor aponeurosis
- Occurs in ~**1/3** of all gamekeeper's thumbs
- **"Yo-yo on a string"** appearance:
  - Yo-yo = balled-up, retracted UCL
  - String = adductor aponeurosis
- Aponeurosis interposition **prevents healing** → chronic laxity, loss of grip, DJD

### Why It Matters
- Treatment within **first 3 weeks** has better outcome than delayed treatment
- Physical examination is **inaccurate** for Stener lesion → MRI warranted
- Non-Stener: May heal with immobilization
- **Stener**: Requires **surgical repair** (aponeurosis prevents ligament from reaching bone)

---

## Key Points
- UCL of thumb: Deep to adductor aponeurosis normally
- Stener lesion: UCL superficial to aponeurosis = cannot heal → needs surgery
- "Yo-yo on a string" = Stener lesion on MRI
- 1/3 avulsion fracture, 2/3 ligament only; 1/3 develop Stener
- Image with small FOV (6-8 cm) coronal to thumb
