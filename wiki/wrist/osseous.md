# Wrist Osseous Structures

## Ulnar Variance {#ulnar-variance}

### Definition
- Relationship of distal articular surfaces of radius and ulna on coronal images
- **Neutral**: Same level (or within 2 mm)
- **Negative** (ulnar minus): Ulna >2 mm proximal to radius
- **Positive** (ulnar plus): Ulna distal to radius
- **Pitfall**: Curved ulnar surface — do NOT diagnose negative variance unless ulna shorter on **all** coronal images

### Associations

| Ulnar Variance | Associated Pathology |
|---------------|---------------------|
| **Positive** | TFC tears, [[#ulnolunate-impaction\|ulnolunate impaction]] |
| **Negative** | [[#kienbock\|Kienböck's disease]] (lunate osteonecrosis) |

---

## Ulnolunate Impaction Syndrome {#ulnolunate-impaction}
- Chronic abutment of distal ulna against proximal lunate
- Strong association with **positive ulnar variance** (congenital or post-distal radial fracture)
- MRI shows marrow abnormalities when **radiographs are normal**:
  - Cartilage destruction
  - Subchondral bone marrow edema
  - Subchondral cyst formation / sclerosis in proximal lunate or ulnar head
  - Associated **TFC tears**

### Stylocarpal Impaction
- After ulnar styloid fracture malunion → deformed/elongated styloid affects triquetrum
- T2: Bone marrow edema or cystic change in triquetrum + elongated ulnar styloid

## Occult Fractures

### Scaphoid Fracture
- **Most commonly fractured** carpal bone
- **~16%** not evident on initial radiographs → MRI excellent for detection
- Complications: Delayed union, osteonecrosis of proximal fragment
- Limited trauma-screening MRI sufficient if fracture is only concern

### Other Carpal Fractures
- May appear as marrow edema ± fracture line
- **Hook of hamate fracture**: Easily missed on coronal; check **axial T1** images
- Marrow edema without fracture line does NOT indicate less significant injury — true fracture may appear on follow-up radiographs

---

## Physeal Injuries
- Distal radial/ulnar physeal injuries (acute or chronic)
- Salter-Harris types IV and V → risk of **physeal bridge** formation → growth disturbance + deformity
- **Physeal bridge** on MRI: Focal low signal traversing cartilaginous physis from metaphysis to epiphysis
- Best shown on GRE or STIR sequences

### Gymnast Wrist
- Chronic stress to distal radial physis in young competitive gymnasts/cheerleaders
- Essentially **Salter I stress injury**
- MRI: Widening and irregularity of physis (unossified cartilage) ± adjacent marrow edema
- May be detected earlier with MRI than radiographs

## Osteonecrosis {#osteonecrosis}

### Scaphoid Osteonecrosis {#scaphoid-avn}
- **Proximal pole** at risk after fracture
- Blood supply enters distally and flows proximally (retrograde) → fracture disrupts flow
- Assessment of proximal fragment viability critical for surgical planning

| T1 Signal | T2 Signal | Interpretation |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| **Normal fat** (bright) | Intermediate | **Viable** (normal) |
| **Low** | **Low** | **Osteonecrosis** |
| **Low** | **High** | Questionable — may be viable or nonviable (edema, healing, ischemia) |

- Practical rule: If decreased T1 signal is **as dark as cortical bone** or **darker than muscle** → highly predictive of osteonecrosis at surgery

### Kienböck's Disease (Lunate Osteonecrosis) {#kienbock}
- Causes: Repetitive trauma, acute fracture, **ulnar minus variance**
- Males > females; usually manual laborers
- Blood supply: Tenuous (end arteries) + central position → strong compressive forces

### Kienböck's MRI Patterns

| Pattern | Diagnosis |
|---------|-----------|
| Low T1 + Low T2 involving **entire lunate** | **Diagnostic** for osteonecrosis |
| Partial involvement or low T1 + high T2 | Less definite — DDx includes early AVN, intraosseous ganglion, ulnolunate impaction |

- High T2 signal = **earlier stage**, better outcome
- DDx for focal lunate lesions: Intraosseous ganglion cyst, ulnolunate impaction (both focal, high T2)

## Congenital Osseous Lesions

### Os Styloideum
- Common accessory ossicle on **dorsum of wrist** near 2nd-3rd metacarpal bases
- May cause pain from: DJD with underlying capitate/trapezoid, direct injury, overlying bursitis/ganglion
- Can be confused with tumor or fracture

### Carpal Coalition
- Most common: **Lunotriquetral** coalition (osseous, fibrous, or cartilaginous)
- Fibrocartilaginous type: Associated marrow edema or cystic changes mimicking DJD

### Type II Lunate
- Extra facet articulating with proximal **hamate** (vs type I = capitate only)
- May lead to cartilage loss of hamate with marrow edema/subchondral cysts in proximal hamate

---

## Key Points
- Ulnar variance: Positive → TFC tears / ulnolunate impaction; Negative → Kienböck's
- Scaphoid: Proximal pole AVN after fracture; low T1 + low T2 = osteonecrosis
- Kienböck's: Low T1 + low T2 of entire lunate = diagnostic; partial/mixed = consider DDx
- 16% of scaphoid fractures occult on XR → MRI for detection
- Hook of hamate fracture: Check axial images
- Gymnast wrist: Salter I stress injury of distal radial physis
