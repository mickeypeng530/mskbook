# Glenoid Labrum

## Anatomy
- **Structure**: Redundant fold of joint capsule, **fibrocartilaginous** tissue
- **Attachment**: Rim of glenoid of scapula
- **Function**: Deepens glenoid fossa; attachment site for biceps tendon and [[instability#gh-ligaments|glenohumeral ligaments]]
- Labrum and ligamentous collagen fibers intertwine — injury more likely at **labrum-bone junction** (detachment) than labroligamentous junction

## MRI Appearance
- **Signal**: Uniformly low on all pulse sequences
- **Best planes**: Superior labrum → coronal oblique; anterior/posterior → axial
- **Shape** (all normal): Triangular (most common) > round > flat > absent
- May show intermediate signal from **magic angle** or **myxoid degeneration** with aging

### Sequence Selection
| Condition | Best Sequence |
|-----------|--------------|
| Joint effusion present | FSE T2 FS |
| No effusion | FSE PD FS or GRE |
| Best overall | MR arthrography |

## Normal Variants

### Sublabral Foramen
- Anterosuperior labrum **detached** from glenoid
- Labrum **reattaches at mid-portion** — key distinction from avulsed labrum (which does NOT reattach)
- No clinical significance

### Buford Complex
- **Congenital absence** of anterosuperior labrum + **markedly thickened MGHL**
- Thick MGHL blends with anterior capsule on inferior axial images
- No clinical significance

### Sublabral Recess (Superior Sulcus)
- Synovial recess between glenoid rim and superior labrum
- Curves **medially with the glenoid** ("follows the flow")
- SLAP tears extend **laterally** ("against the flow")

### Undercutting Cartilage
- Hyaline cartilage between labrum and glenoid cortex
- Lower signal than gadolinium — not labral detachment
- Also "follows the flow" of glenoid contour

## Common Pathology
- [[instability#bankart|Bankart lesion]] — anteroinferior labral tear
- [[slap-lesion]] — superior labral tear
- Posterosuperior labral fraying/tear — see [[rotator-cuff]] (internal impingement)

## Pitfalls
- **MGHL** on axial may mimic torn anterior labral fragment
- Sublabral foramen mimics anterior labral tear
- Buford complex mimics pathology
- Superior sulcus mimics SLAP — use the "follow the glenoid contour" rule
- Myxoid degeneration mimics intrasubstance tear
