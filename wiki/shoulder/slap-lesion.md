# SLAP Lesion

## Definition
**Superior Labrum Anterior to Posterior** tear — injury to the superior [[labrum]] at the biceps anchor.

## Mechanism / Etiology
- **Compression**: Fall on outstretched hand (FOOSH) with shoulder abducted
- **Traction**: Sudden pull on arm, overhead throwing (peel-back mechanism)
- **Repetitive overhead activity**: Pitchers, swimmers, tennis players

## Classification {#slap-types}

| Type | Description | Biceps Anchor |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| **I** | Superior labral **fraying and degeneration** | Intact |
| **II** | Superior labral and **biceps anchor detachment** | **Detached** — most common |
| **III** | **Bucket-handle tear** of superior labrum | Intact |
| **IV** | Bucket-handle tear extending into **biceps tendon** | Involved |

- Refs: Andrews JR et al. Am J Sports Med. 1985. Snyder SJ et al. Arthroscopy. 1990.

## MRI Findings
- **Best plane**: Coronal oblique
- High signal at superior labrum extending between labrum and glenoid
- **Type II**: Linear high signal undercutting biceps anchor, extending laterally
- **Type III**: Displaced labral fragment in superior joint recess
- **Type IV**: Tear extends into biceps tendon substance
- MR arthrography significantly improves detection

### Key Rule: "With the Flow" vs "Against the Flow"
- **SLAP tear**: Signal extends **laterally** away from glenoid ("against the flow")
- **Normal sublabral recess**: Curves **medially** with glenoid ("with the flow")

## Paralabral Cysts
- Arise from superior or posterior labral tears
- Extend into suprascapular or spinoglenoid notch → [[nerve-entrapment]]
- **T1**: Low signal, well-defined, lobular, septated
- **T2**: High signal
- **Will NOT fill** with gadolinium on arthrography — need T2 FS to detect

## Associated Conditions
- [[nerve-entrapment|Suprascapular nerve entrapment]] via paralabral cyst
- [[biceps-long-head]] — especially Type IV
- Internal impingement in overhead athletes

## Pitfalls
- Sublabral recess vs. SLAP II — use the "flow" rule
- Type I may be indistinguishable from age-related degeneration
- Paralabral cysts invisible on T1 arthrographic sequences — always include T2 FS
- Magic angle effect at superior labrum

## Key Points
- Type II is most common and clinically significant
- "Against the flow" = SLAP tear; "with the flow" = normal variant
- Always look for paralabral cysts when SLAP identified
- MR arthrography is the gold standard
