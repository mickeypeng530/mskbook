# Shoulder Avascular Necrosis

## Definition
Osteonecrosis of the humeral head — **second most common site** after femoral head.

## Etiology
- Corticosteroid use
- Marrow infiltrative disorders (e.g., sickle cell disease)
- Fracture of surgical neck of humerus

## Location
- Most frequently involves **superomedial** aspect of humeral head

## MRI Findings
- **T1**: Well-defined, low signal, **serpiginous line or arc** delineating necrosis
- Center of lesion: Variable signal, most frequently **fat signal**
- Permits **early detection** before radiographic changes

## Differential Diagnosis
- Occult fracture — similar appearance but rare in superomedial location; acute fracture has abundant surrounding edema
- Bone contusion — ill-defined, reticulated pattern (not serpiginous)

## Key Points
- Second most common AVN site (after femoral head)
- Classic: Serpiginous low T1 line, superomedial humeral head
- Always consider in patients on steroids or with marrow disorders
