# Rotator Cuff

## Anatomy

The rotator cuff is composed of four tendons forming a continuous tendinous hood:

| Tendon | Origin | Insertion | Function | Innervation |
|--------|--------|-----------|----------|-------------|
| **Supraspinatus** | Supraspinous fossa | Superior greater tuberosity | Abduction (with deltoid) | Suprascapular n. |
| **Infraspinatus** | Infraspinous fossa | Posterior greater tuberosity | External rotation | Suprascapular n. |
| **Teres minor** | Lateral scapular border | Inferior greater tuberosity | External rotation | Axillary n. |
| **Subscapularis** | Subscapular fossa | Lesser tuberosity | Internal rotation | Upper/lower subscapular n. |

### Structure
- Laminated structure: joint capsule + ligaments + four tendons
- Tendons have cylindrical and flat portions that fan out and **interdigitate** to form a continuous hood
- **No synovial sheaths** or paratenon surrounding the tendons
- **Coracohumeral ligament** lies superficial to the capsule and supraspinatus/infraspinatus tendons

### MRI Appearance by Plane
- **Coronal oblique**: Supraspinatus (horizontal, just lateral to AC joint) and infraspinatus (oblique, ~45°) seen longitudinally
- **Axial**: Subscapularis seen longitudinally
- **Sagittal oblique**: All four muscles in cross section; best for assessing muscle bulk and fatty infiltration
- Supraspinatus inserts into **fibrocartilage** (not hyaline cartilage) on greater tuberosity
- With internal rotation, **bicipital groove** is a landmark for the leading edge of the supraspinatus

## Pathology: Impingement

### External (Subacromial) Impingement
Compression of supraspinatus beneath the **coracoacromial arch** (acromion + coracoacromial ligament + AC joint).

**Causes**:
- Abnormal acromial morphology (see Bigliani below)
- AC joint osteophytes
- Thickened coracoacromial ligament
- Os acromiale (unfused acromial apophysis — best seen on axial)
- Secondary to [[instability]] (superior humeral head migration)

### Subcoracoid Impingement
- Narrowing between coracoid and humerus → subscapularis compression
- Congenital or post-fracture/surgery

### Internal Impingement
- See [[posterosuperior-impingement]] — infraspinatus impinged between humeral head and posterior glenoid during abduction/external rotation

### Neer Impingement Stages
| Stage | Description | Age | Treatment |
|-------|-------------|-----|-----------|
| I | Edema and hemorrhage | < 25 | Conservative |
| II | Fibrosis and tendinitis | 25-40 | Decompression if conservative fails |
| III | Bone spurs and tendon rupture | > 40 | Surgery |

### Bigliani Acromion Morphology {#bigliani}
| Type | Morphology | Impingement Risk |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| **I** | Flat | Lowest |
| **II** | Curved | Intermediate |
| **III** | Hooked | **Highest** |

- Assessed on **sagittal oblique** images (undersurface of acromion)
- Ref: Bigliani LU et al. Orthop Trans. 1986;10:228.

## Pathology: Rotator Cuff Tears

### Partial-Thickness Tears

| Type | Description | Frequency |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| **Articular-sided** | Disruption of joint-side fibers | Most common side |
| **Bursal-sided** | Disruption of bursal-side fibers | Much less common |
| **Rim rent (PASTA/PASSTA)** | Insertional fiber avulsion from greater tuberosity | **~70% of all partial tears** |

- Tears start in the **anterior** portion of distal supraspinatus and propagate **posteriorly**
- Tears at the "critical zone" (1-1.5 cm proximal to insertion) are **not as common as previously believed**
- Detection improves with joint fluid or MR arthrography
- MRI is **less sensitive** for partial than full-thickness tears

### Full-Thickness Tears
- **Direct sign**: Tendon discontinuity with fluid signal traversing from articular to bursal surface
- On MR arthrography: Gadolinium in joint AND subacromial/subdeltoid bursa
- **Sensitivity and specificity > 90%**
- Usually involve distal fibers near attachment or critical zone

### Massive Cuff Tears
- Full-thickness tears of **multiple tendons** with retraction and atrophy
- Large GH joint–bursal communication
- **Geyser sign**: Synovial cyst through degenerated AC joint
- **Rotator cuff arthropathy**: Severe GH OA, superior humeral migration, subchondral cysts

### Secondary Signs of Tears
- Fluid in subacromial/subdeltoid bursa
- Superior migration of humeral head
- Muscle atrophy / fatty infiltration
- Subacromial degenerative changes

### What to Report
1. Which tendon(s) involved
2. Partial vs. full-thickness
3. Tear size (Cofield)
4. Degree of tendon retraction
5. Quality of tendon margins
6. Muscle atrophy / fatty infiltration (Goutallier)
7. Associated osseous abnormalities

## Classification: Tear Size — Cofield {#cofield}

| Size | Greatest Dimension |
|------|-------------------|
| **Small** | < 1 cm |
| **Medium** | 1-3 cm |
| **Large** | 3-5 cm |
| **Massive** | > 5 cm (usually multiple tendons) |

- Measure greatest dimension on coronal oblique + sagittal oblique
- Ref: Cofield RH. Surg Gynecol Obstet. 1982;154:667-672.

## Classification: Fatty Infiltration — Goutallier {#goutallier}

| Grade | Criteria | Significance |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **0** | No fatty streaks | Normal |
| **1** | Some fatty streaks | Minimal |
| **2** | Less fat than muscle | Moderate |
| **3** | Equal fat and muscle | Significant — may affect repair |
| **4** | More fat than muscle | Severe — poor surgical prognosis |

- Assessed on **T1W without fat suppression** (sagittal oblique or coronal oblique)
- Grade 3-4 may be **irreversible** — favor conservative treatment or tendon transfer
- **Pitfall**: Fatty infiltration easily missed on fat-suppressed images
- Ref: Goutallier D et al. Clin Orthop Relat Res. 1994;304:78-83.

## Postoperative Rotator Cuff

- Avoid **gradient echo** sequences (blooming from hemosiderin/metal) — use FSE
- Postop tendon is **intermediate signal** — this is normal (granulation tissue, degeneration)
- Diagnose retear only with **fluid-signal gap on T2W**
- Fluid in subacromial bursa may be present even with intact repair (not watertight)
- Scar tissue: High T2 in first year → intermediate/low signal after 1 year
- Contrast not particularly helpful postoperatively

---

## Pitfalls
- **Internal rotation** obscures anterior supraspinatus rim rent tears — always check anterior coronal images near bicipital groove
- **Magic angle effect** at 55° to B0 — elevated signal on short TE (T1, PD), disappears on T2
- Partial volume averaging between biceps fluid and anterior supraspinatus on sagittal images
- Os acromiale confused with AC joint on axial images
- Subacromial fluid alone is nonspecific

## Key Points
- **Rim rent tear** (PASTA) is the most common partial tear type (70%)
- Tears start anteriorly, propagate posteriorly
- Full-thickness: Fluid traversing entire tendon + bursal communication
- Always assess muscle quality (Goutallier) — determines surgical planning
- Type III (hooked) acromion = highest impingement risk
- MR arthrography improves detection of both partial and full-thickness tears
