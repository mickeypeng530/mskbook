# Shoulder MRI Protocol

## Coil
- Surface coil required for high-resolution imaging

## Patient Position
- Supine, arm at side
- Neutral position or slight external rotation
- Internal rotation shifts bicipital groove posteriorly — may obscure anterior supraspinatus tears

## Sequences

### Conventional MRI
| Sequence | Plane | Purpose |
|----------|-------|---------|
| T1W | Sagittal oblique | Cuff muscle size, fatty infiltration |
| FSE T2 FS | Sagittal oblique | Fluid/edema, bursae, cuff tears, biceps tendon |
| FSE T2 FS | Coronal oblique | Supraspinatus/infraspinatus tears, superior labrum |
| FSE T2 FS | Axial | Anterior/posterior labrum, biceps in groove, subscapularis |
| FSE PD FS | Axial | Labrum (better if no joint effusion) |

### MR Arthrography
| Sequence | Plane | Purpose |
|----------|-------|---------|
| T1W FS | Coronal oblique | Labral tears, cuff tears with gadolinium |
| T1W FS | Sagittal oblique | Rotator interval, glenohumeral ligaments |
| T1W FS | Axial | Labrum, capsule, glenohumeral ligaments |
| FSE T2 FS | Coronal oblique | Noncommunicating fluid (paralabral cysts) |
| FSE T2 FS | Axial | Noncommunicating fluid collections |
| T1W (no FS) | Sagittal oblique | Muscle atrophy assessment (easily missed on FS) |

### Arthrogram Solution
- 0.1 mL gadolinium + 20 mL normal saline + 3 mL iodinated contrast
- Inject ~10-12 mL into joint under fluoroscopic guidance

### IV Gadolinium
- Used for suspected infection or inflammatory arthritis
- Assess synovitis and periarticular fluid collections

## Image Orientation
- **FOV**: 12 cm, slice thickness 3-4 mm
- **Coronal oblique**: Parallel to supraspinatus tendon (seen on axial), or perpendicular to glenoid articular surface
- **Axial**: From top of acromion to bottom of GH joint
- **Sagittal oblique**: Parallel to glenoid articular surface, from scapular neck to lateral humerus

## Key Structures to Evaluate

### Coronal Oblique
- [ ] Supraspinatus muscle and tendon (longitudinal)
- [ ] Infraspinatus muscle and tendon (longitudinal)
- [ ] Acromioclavicular joint
- [ ] Acromion morphology
- [ ] Glenohumeral joint
- [ ] Subacromial/subdeltoid bursa
- [ ] Superior and inferior labrum

### Sagittal Oblique
- [ ] Supraspinatus (cross section)
- [ ] Infraspinatus (cross section)
- [ ] Teres minor (cross section)
- [ ] Subscapularis (cross section)
- [ ] Long head of biceps (cross section, proximal)
- [ ] Rotator interval
- [ ] Acromion and coracoacromial ligament
- [ ] Glenohumeral ligaments

### Axial
- [ ] Long head of biceps in bicipital groove (cross section)
- [ ] Subscapularis muscle and tendon (longitudinal)
- [ ] Anterior and posterior labrum
- [ ] Capsule
- [ ] Glenohumeral ligaments

## Common Pitfalls
- Internal rotation of humerus obscures anterior supraspinatus insertion — check bicipital groove on anterior coronal images
- FSE T2 FS shows labrum better with joint effusion; PD FS (or GRE) better without effusion
- Gradient echo sequences should be avoided postoperatively (blooming artifact from hemosiderin/metal)
- Subcoracoid bursa injection may be mistaken for successful joint injection during arthrography

## Tips
- T2 FS sequence: Best for labrum evaluation when joint effusion is present
- PD FS sequence: Best for labrum when no effusion
- At least one T2W FS sequence should be included in MR arthrography to detect noncommunicating cysts
- Muscle atrophy is easily missed on fat-suppressed images — include one non-FS T1W sequence
