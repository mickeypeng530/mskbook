# Posterosuperior Impingement (GIRD)

## Definition
Impingement of the posterior supraspinatus and primarily the **infraspinatus tendon** between the humeral head and the **posterior glenoid rim** during overhead movements. Also known as **internal impingement** or **Glenohumeral Internal Rotational Deficit (GIRD)**.

## Pathophysiology
- Occurs in the **late cocking phase** of throwing — maximum **abduction and external rotation**
- The greater tuberosity abuts the posterosuperior glenoid
- Repetitive microtrauma to infraspinatus tendon, posterosuperior labrum, and adjacent bone
- Common in overhead athletes: pitchers, tennis players, swimmers

## Components
1. **Infraspinatus (and supraspinatus) undersurface tendon tears** — articular-sided partial tears
2. **Posterosuperior labral tear** — fraying or frank tear
3. **Humeral cysts** — degenerative cysts at posterior greater tuberosity near infraspinatus insertion

## Clinical Presentation
- **Posterior shoulder pain**
- May have associated **anterior shoulder instability** (from chronic stretching of anterior structures)
- Symptoms exacerbated by abduction and external rotation

## MRI Findings

| Structure | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| Infraspinatus tendon | Undersurface (articular-sided) partial tear or fraying |
| Supraspinatus tendon | May also have undersurface tear |
| Posterosuperior labrum | Blunting, fraying, or tear |
| Posterior humeral head | Degenerative cysts near infraspinatus insertion |

## Associated Conditions
- [[slap-lesion]] — may coexist in overhead athletes
- [[rotator-cuff-tear]] — partial articular-sided tears of infraspinatus/supraspinatus
- Anterior instability — chronic stretching from repetitive motion
- [[bankart-lesion|Bennett lesion]] — posterior capsular avulsion from deceleration

## Differential Diagnosis
- External (subacromial) [[shoulder-impingement]] — anterior/lateral pain, different mechanism
- [[rotator-cuff-tear]] from other causes
- Posterior labral tear from posterior instability

## Key Points
- Classic triad: Infraspinatus undersurface tear + posterosuperior labral tear + humeral cysts
- Occurs with abduction and external rotation (late cocking phase)
- Primarily affects overhead athletes
- Posterior shoulder pain with possible anterior instability
- Look for articular-sided partial tears of infraspinatus/supraspinatus
