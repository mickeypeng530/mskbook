# Suprascapular Nerve Entrapment

## Definition
Compression of the suprascapular nerve as it courses through the suprascapular or spinoglenoid notch, causing pain, weakness, and muscle atrophy.

## Anatomy
- Suprascapular nerve runs superior to scapula in anteroposterior direction
- **Suprascapular notch**: Nerve innervates both **supraspinatus and infraspinatus**
- **Spinoglenoid notch**: Nerve innervates **infraspinatus only**
- Provides sensory innervation to AC and GH joints

## Mechanism / Etiology
- **Most common cause**: Ganglion (paralabral) cyst from labral tear (especially [[slap-lesion]])
- Other causes:
  - Large veins (varices) in spinoglenoid notch
  - Tumors
  - Fracture of scapula

## MRI Findings

### The Mass
- **Ganglion cyst**: Well-defined, round/oval, septated
  - T1: Low signal
  - T2: High signal
  - With IV gadolinium: Low signal center, thin peripheral enhancement
- Location determines which muscles are affected:
  - **Suprascapular notch** → supraspinatus AND infraspinatus atrophy
  - **Spinoglenoid notch** → infraspinatus only

### Muscle Changes
| Phase | T1 Finding | T2 Finding |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| Subacute (edema) | Normal | **High signal** in muscle |
| Chronic (atrophy) | **High signal** (fat infiltration) | Variable |

### Associated Findings
- Look for underlying labral tear (especially posterior or superior)
- Cyst arising from [[slap-lesion]] is classic

## Clinical Presentation
- Initially described in male weightlifters
- Shoulder pain → weakness → muscle atrophy
- Pattern depends on level of compression

## Treatment
- Percutaneous drainage + corticosteroid injection (alternative to surgery)
- Surgical removal of cyst + labral repair if associated labral tear

## Differential Diagnosis
- [[parsonage-turner-syndrome]] — acute brachial neuritis (no mass, sudden onset)
- Quadrilateral space syndrome (teres minor ± deltoid only)
- Rotator cuff tear with secondary atrophy (no mass in notch)

## Pitfalls
- Paralabral cysts may NOT fill with gadolinium on arthrography — need T2 FS to detect
- Always search for the underlying labral tear
- Isolated infraspinatus atrophy → spinoglenoid notch; combined supra + infraspinatus → suprascapular notch

## Key Points
- Most commonly caused by ganglion cyst from labral tear
- Location of compression determines muscle involvement pattern
- Suprascapular notch = supraspinatus + infraspinatus; spinoglenoid notch = infraspinatus only
- Subacute = T2 muscle edema; chronic = T1 fatty atrophy
- Always look for labral tear as the source of the cyst
